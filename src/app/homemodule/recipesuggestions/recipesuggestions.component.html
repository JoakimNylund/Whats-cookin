Berry of the week: Blueberry

Blueberries are your friends that you can not help but eat.


People have been eating blueberries for more than 13,000 years and for good reason. It is low in calories but high in fibre, vitamin C and vitamin K. The silvery sheen (or “bloom”) found on the skin of blueberries is a naturally occurring compound that helps protect the fruit. That is why you should only wash blueberries just before you’re going to eat them. The berries should be stored in the refrigerator and will keep fresh for up to 10 days. Pasta al limone

Quick lunch? Try this italian classic.


A simple but sophisticated italian recipe. A dish from the south of Italy, traditionally cooked with Amalfi lemons. It is of no surprise that lemons are a traditional ingredient in the local cuisine in this part of Italy. They are used in both sweet and savoury dishes, the most famous of the latter is probably pasta al limone! In Amalfi, they even put lemon in their coffee! Find our pasta al limone recipes here.
